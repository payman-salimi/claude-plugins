# Body Recomposition

The honest truth: **simultaneous muscle gain and fat loss is hard at most experience levels.** It works dramatically for some populations and slowly for others. Set expectations correctly before programming.

## Who can recomp — and how fast

| Population | Recomp potential | Realistic rate |
|---|---|---|
| Untrained beginners | Excellent — "newbie gains" | Significant change in 12–24 weeks |
| Returning lifters (after long break) | Very good | Strong recovery of past muscle while losing fat |
| Overweight clients new to strength | Excellent | Both happen simultaneously and visibly |
| Intermediate, lean (~15% BF M / ~22% F) | Slow but possible | Months of subtle change |
| Advanced, lean | Very slow / minimal | Most should pick gain OR lose, not both |
| Detrained athletes | Excellent (muscle memory) | Rapid recomposition |

**If the client is advanced and lean, be honest:** they're better off picking one goal at a time. Run a hypertrophy phase, then a cut, then back. They'll get better total results than slogging through a recomp.

For everyone else, recomp is real and worth pursuing.

## The two pillars

### 1. Maintenance calories or very small deficit

Body recomp lives at the edge of caloric balance:
- **Beginners and overweight clients**: small deficit (10–15% below maintenance) — they recomp easily even in a slight deficit
- **Intermediates**: maintenance ± 100 cal — toggle between slight surplus on training days, slight deficit on rest days
- **Advanced**: maintenance — recomp is so slow at this level that any deficit costs muscle gain

### 2. High protein

This is the non-negotiable:
- **2.0–2.4 g/kg bodyweight per day** — higher than cutting, higher than bulking
- Spread across 4–5 meals, 0.4 g/kg per meal
- Protein has the highest thermic effect of any macro AND is the substrate for muscle growth — both pillars served by one nutrient

## Training: bias toward hypertrophy programming

Recomp training looks like a hypertrophy program with slightly more conditioning. Volume drives the muscle growth signal even when calories are tight.

### Volume

Hypertrophy-style: 12–18 hard sets per muscle group per week, building over a block, deloading, restarting.

### Frequency

Each muscle hit 2–3x per week. Upper/lower 4x or PPL 6x both work.

### Intensity

Working sets at RPE 7–8. Don't push to RPE 10 — recovery is tighter when calories are limited, and pushing to failure costs you.

### Exercise selection

Lean toward high-tension compound movements that recruit multiple muscles per set:
- Squat, deadlift, bench, row, pull-up, overhead press, hip thrust, RDL
- Plus targeted accessory work for weak points

### Rep ranges

Mostly 6–12. Occasional 5s on compounds for strength preservation. Occasional 15+ for finishers and pump work.

## Sample 4-day recomp template

**Day 1 — Upper (heavy bias)**
- Bench press 4x6 @ RPE 8
- Pull-up or weighted pull-up 4x6
- Overhead press 3x8
- Cable row 3x10
- Lateral raise 3x12
- Curl 2x10
- Tricep pushdown 2x12

**Day 2 — Lower (heavy bias)**
- Squat 4x6 @ RPE 8
- RDL 3x8
- Bulgarian split squat 3x8/leg
- Leg curl 3x12
- Calf raise 3x12
- Plank 3x30 sec

**Day 3 — Z2 cardio (45 min)** + walking

**Day 4 — Upper (volume bias)**
- Incline DB press 4x10
- Bent-over row 4x8
- Lat pulldown 3x10
- Cable fly 3x12
- Rear delt fly 3x15
- Hammer curl 2x12
- Skull crusher 2x10

**Day 5 — Lower (volume bias)**
- Front squat 4x8
- Hip thrust 3x10
- Walking lunge 3x10/leg
- Leg extension 3x12
- Hamstring curl 3x12
- Hanging leg raise 3x10

**Day 6 — Optional Z2 + walking**

**Day 7 — Rest**

## Cardio for recomp

Moderate dose — not as heavy as a pure cut, but more than a pure hypertrophy phase:

- 2x Z2 sessions of 30–45 min
- 1x light interval session of 15–20 min
- Daily walking habit (8000–10000 steps)

This adds enough to the deficit without blocking recovery from lifting.

## Tracking recomp progress

The scale doesn't tell the whole story for recomp. Track multiple things:

- **Weight** (5–7 day average): may stay flat or move very slowly. That's expected and good.
- **Tape measurements**: waist, hip, chest, arms — every 2 weeks. The waist comes down, arms/chest go up. That's the real signal.
- **Photos**: weekly, same lighting, same pose. Recomp shows in photos before it shows on the scale.
- **Strength on big lifts**: must be moving up or holding. Drop in strength = under-eating.
- **Body fat estimate** (DEXA, calipers, BIA): every 4–8 weeks. Trends matter more than single numbers.

## Common recomp pitfalls

### Pitfall: too aggressive with the deficit

The temptation: "I want to lose fat, so eat less." But muscle growth needs adequate calories. A 500+ deficit kills the muscle-building side of the equation. Stay close to maintenance.

### Pitfall: not enough protein

Recomp lives or dies on protein. 1.6 g/kg is too low. Push to 2.0+ even if it feels like a lot. Whey protein, Greek yogurt, eggs, lean meat, cottage cheese, fish.

### Pitfall: chasing the scale

Recomp clients see the scale stay flat for weeks and assume nothing's working. The mirror, the tape, the photos, and the lifts tell the actual story. Coach this expectation in week 1.

### Pitfall: skimping on training

"I'll lift twice a week and run more." Wrong direction. Recomp is a muscle-building phase masquerading as a cut. Lift 3–4x/week minimum.

### Pitfall: thinking recomp is forever

Recomp is slow. After 12–20 weeks, evaluate. If progress is meaningful, continue. If it's stalled (and the client is no longer in the easy-recomp populations), shift to a focused cut or focused gain phase. The body comp will continue to improve over multiple cycles.

## Phasing recomp

A practical model for non-beginners:

- **Weeks 1–6**: slight deficit, hypertrophy training. Watch the scale, tape, lifts.
- **Weeks 7–8**: maintenance calories (refeed phase). Lifts often jump here.
- **Weeks 9–14**: slight deficit again. Continue hypertrophy work.
- **Weeks 15–16**: maintenance phase. Reassess body comp.
- **Continue or transition** based on results.

## Communication to the client

> "Recomp works, but it's the slowest path. We're going to lift like you're trying to build muscle, eat at or just under maintenance, and hit protein hard. The scale isn't going to tell you what's happening — the photos and the lifts will. Trust the process for 12 weeks before judging it. If you want faster results, we pick one goal — gain or lose — and chase it directly. I can support either call."

## What this skill does NOT do

- Does not promise dramatic results in advanced lean clients (they should pick gain or lose)
- Does not work without high protein and consistent training
- Does not run long-term cuts (that's `fat-loss`)
- Does not run aggressive bulking (that's `hypertrophy`)
- Does not skip strength tracking — losing strength = losing the recomp
