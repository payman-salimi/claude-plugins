# Maintenance

Maintenance is underrated. Most clients don't need to be in growth mode forever — and constantly chasing PRs is a fast track to burnout, injury, and overtraining.

The good news: **maintaining what you've built takes a lot less than building it.** Minimum effective dose is real, and substantial.

## Who this is for

- Clients between cut/bulk phases who want to hold the line
- Athletes in their off-season or in-season competition
- Busy life chapters (new job, new baby, travel-heavy seasons) where holding is the right move
- Clients at or near their genetic ceiling who can't keep adding without diminishing returns
- Older clients prioritizing longevity and joint health over PRs
- Anyone who's reached a body composition or strength level they love

It's a feature, not a failure. Frame it that way.

## The maintenance volume question

The research is clear: muscle and strength can be maintained with **roughly 1/3 the volume that built them**, as long as intensity stays high. So if a client built their physique on 18 hard sets/muscle/week, they can hold it on 6–10 hard sets/muscle/week.

**Maintenance volume:** 6–10 hard sets per muscle group per week.

That's it. The whole hour-and-a-half-six-days-a-week schedule can compress to 3 sessions of 45 minutes if needed.

## Frequency

3 sessions/week is enough to maintain most physiques. 2 sessions/week works for shorter periods (4–6 weeks at most before slow regression starts).

## Intensity rules

- **Keep intensity HIGH** — RPE 7–9 on working sets. This is the lever you cannot drop.
- Volume can drop. Frequency can drop. **Intensity preserves muscle and strength.** Lift like you mean it on each set, even if you're doing fewer.

## A 3-day maintenance template

Full-body, hits every major muscle group 3x/week with low total volume.

**Day A**
- Squat 3x5 @ RPE 8
- Bench press 3x5 @ RPE 8
- Bent-over row 3x5 @ RPE 8
- Pull-up 2xAMRAP
- Plank 2x30 sec

**Day B**
- Trap bar deadlift 3x5 @ RPE 8
- Overhead press 3x5
- Lat pulldown 3x8
- Lateral raise 2x12
- Hanging leg raise 2x10

**Day C**
- Front squat 3x5
- Incline DB press 3x8
- Cable row 3x10
- RDL 2x6
- Curl 2x10
- Tricep extension 2x10

That's it. 5 lifts per session, 30–45 minutes. Maintains everything if intensity is honest.

## Strength maintenance

Strength can be maintained with even less:
- 1–2 sessions/week per lift
- 2–3 working sets at RPE 7–9
- Top set in the 3–5 rep range

For the busiest clients, a "minimum effective dose" template:

**Mon**: Squat 3x5, Bench 3x5
**Wed**: Deadlift 2x3, Overhead Press 3x5, Pull-up 3xAMRAP
**Fri**: optional volume / accessories

Total time: ~90 min per week. Holds strength for years.

## Cardio in maintenance

Don't drop cardio. Keep:
- 2x Z2 sessions of 30–45 min
- Optional 1 short interval session
- Daily walking habit

Cardio in maintenance has cardiovascular and metabolic benefits independent of body comp.

## Calorie target

**Maintenance calories** — bodyweight (kg) × 30–35 for moderate activity, adjust based on actual scale stability.

If weight is drifting up: small reduction.
If drifting down: small increase.

The whole point is the scale stays in a 2–3 lb band over months.

Protein: 1.4–1.8 g/kg. Lower than a building or cutting phase, but still elevated.

## When maintenance is the right call

- Coming off a successful cut: maintain 4–8 weeks before the next phase. Body re-adapts, water normalizes, the cut "sticks" psychologically.
- Coming off a bulk: maintain to evaluate what was muscle vs. fat before deciding to cut.
- During life chaos: don't try to push training when work, family, or sleep is in upheaval. Maintenance buys time.
- Plateau resistance: every plateau isn't a problem to solve. Some are an indication to maintain for a block before the next push.
- Long-term sustainable life: training is part of life, not the boss of it. Maintenance phases keep training present without making it consuming.

## Mental framing

Some clients struggle psychologically with "not progressing." Reframe:

- Maintenance ≠ stagnation. It's an active choice to consolidate gains.
- The body adapts to the recent stimulus. Maintenance phases let recent gains "set" before pushing further.
- Lifting heavy 3x/week for 30 years is a more impressive arc than lifting 6x/week for 18 months and burning out.

## Transitioning out

After 4–12 weeks of maintenance, decide the next phase:
- Want to gain more muscle? → `hypertrophy`
- Want to lose fat? → `fat-loss`
- Want both? → `body-recomp`
- Stalled even at maintenance? → `plateau-detection` to diagnose

## Communication to the client

> "Maintenance is a real training phase. Three sessions a week, lift heavy for fewer sets, stay sharp on the big lifts, keep cardio rolling, eat at maintenance. You'll hold everything you built — and probably feel less beat up than you have in a while. We can ride this for as long as it serves you. When you're ready for the next push, we'll know."

## What this skill does NOT do

- Does not run high-volume or peaking blocks (read `hypertrophy` or `elite-athlete`)
- Does not drop intensity (intensity is the maintenance lever)
- Does not eliminate cardio
- Does not last forever — eventually transition into the next phase intentionally
