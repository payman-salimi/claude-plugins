# Mediterranean cuisine

The Mediterranean pattern is the most researched, longest-tracked dietary approach in the world — both the DGA and dozens of randomized trials support it for cardiovascular health, type 2 diabetes prevention, and longevity. When a user picks this cuisine, they're picking something genuinely special.

## Core ingredient palette

**Fats**
- Extra virgin olive oil (the keystone — unrefined, fresh)
- Olives (Kalamata, Castelvetrano, green Cerignola)
- Tahini (sesame seed paste)
- Nuts: almonds, walnuts, pistachios

**Proteins**
- Fish: salmon, sardines, branzino, mackerel, anchovies, tuna
- Seafood: shrimp, octopus, calamari
- Chicken (less central than fish; usually grilled or stewed)
- Lamb (occasional, usually braised or grilled)
- Legumes: chickpeas, lentils (red, brown, French), white beans, fava beans
- Eggs
- Dairy: Greek yogurt, feta, halloumi, ricotta, Pecorino, Manchego

**Carbs**
- Whole grain bread (sourdough, whole wheat pita)
- Bulgur, freekeh, farro, barley
- Brown rice (Spanish bomba for paella)
- Couscous (whole wheat)
- Potatoes (especially Greek-style with lemon)

**Vegetables**
- Tomatoes (fresh, sun-dried, paste — central to the cuisine)
- Cucumber, zucchini, eggplant, bell peppers, artichokes
- Greens: spinach, Swiss chard, dandelion, arugula
- Onion, garlic (essential aromatics)

**Herbs and spices**
- Fresh: parsley, mint, dill, oregano, basil, thyme, rosemary
- Dried: oregano, sumac, za'atar, smoked paprika, saffron, Aleppo pepper
- Acid: lemon juice, red wine vinegar, pomegranate molasses

**Fruit**
- Citrus, figs, dates, pomegranate, grapes, stone fruit

## Signature dishes by region

### Greece
- **Greek salad** (horiatiki) — tomato, cucumber, onion, feta, olives, oregano, olive oil, no lettuce
- **Souvlaki** — grilled marinated chicken or pork skewers
- **Spanakopita** — spinach and feta in phyllo
- **Yemista** — stuffed peppers or tomatoes with rice and herbs
- **Avgolemono** — egg-lemon chicken soup
- **Tzatziki** — yogurt + cucumber + garlic + dill

### Levantine (Lebanon, Syria, Palestine, Israel)
- **Tabbouleh** — bulgur, parsley, mint, tomato, lemon, olive oil
- **Hummus** — chickpeas, tahini, lemon, garlic, olive oil
- **Mujadara** — lentils, rice, fried onions
- **Fattoush** — toasted pita salad with sumac
- **Shakshuka** — eggs poached in tomato + pepper sauce
- **Mansaf** (Jordan) — lamb in fermented yogurt with rice

### Italian (south)
- **Pasta puttanesca** — tomato, olives, capers, anchovy, garlic
- **Caponata** — Sicilian sweet-sour eggplant
- **Pasta e fagioli** — pasta and bean soup
- **Acquacotta** — Tuscan vegetable bread soup

### Spain
- **Paella** — saffron rice with seafood or chicken/rabbit
- **Gazpacho** — cold tomato soup
- **Pisto** — Spanish ratatouille
- **Tapas plates** — manchego, jamón, marinated anchovies, olives

### Turkey
- **Mercimek çorbası** — red lentil soup
- **Köfte** — spiced meatballs
- **İmam bayıldı** — stuffed eggplant
- **Çoban salatası** — shepherd's salad

## Macro patterns

A Mediterranean meal plate generally lands at:
- 40–50% calories from carbs (mostly whole grains and vegetables)
- 30–40% from fats (mostly monounsaturated, from olive oil)
- 20–25% from protein (fish, legumes, dairy, with some lean meats)

This naturally hits the DGA targets and provides plenty of fiber. The challenge for a fat-loss plan: olive oil is calorie-dense (120 kcal/tbsp). Be precise with quantities.

## Technique notes

- **Olive oil quality matters.** A finishing pour of good extra-virgin oil at the end is what gives the cuisine its character. Don't substitute with cheap blends.
- **Tomatoes need salt and time.** A diced tomato sits with salt for 10 minutes before going in a salad — it draws out juice and concentrates flavor.
- **Acid at the end.** Lemon juice and vinegar go on after cooking, never before, to keep their brightness.
- **Lentils don't need soaking.** Brown and green lentils cook in 25 minutes; red lentils in 15. Skip the soak.
- **Yogurt as a sauce base.** Most Mediterranean sauces start with full-fat plain yogurt + lemon + olive oil + an aromatic.

## Suggested weekly anchors

For a Mediterranean-leaning weekly plan, build around:
- 2–3 fish nights (salmon, sardines, branzino, or mackerel)
- 1–2 legume-based nights (lentil soup, chickpea stew, mujadara)
- 1 chicken or lamb night (grilled, with vegetables and grain)
- 1 vegetarian + dairy night (shakshuka, spanakopita, or pasta)
- Lunches built on grain bowls, fattoush, or large salads with feta and olives
- Breakfasts: Greek yogurt + nuts + honey, or eggs + tomato + bread

## What NOT to do

- Do not call generic American "Mediterranean wraps" Mediterranean. The cuisine is regional and specific.
- Do not skimp on olive oil — replacing it with neutral oil destroys the flavor and the health profile.
- Do not pretend feta is interchangeable with mozzarella or that Greek yogurt is the same as American yogurt. Brand and source matter.
- Do not load Mediterranean meals with cheese in a way that pushes saturated fat past 10% of calories. The pattern uses cheese as a flavor accent, not the main protein.
